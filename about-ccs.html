College Profile

College of Computer Studies

Mindanao State University — Iligan Institute of Technology

A Center of Excellence in Information and Communication Technology, producing highly competitive graduates imbued with professional values for the local and international ICT industry.

Vision, Mission & Goals

The guiding principles of the College of Computer Studies

Vision

A Center of Excellence in Information and Communication Technology manned by motivated and highly competent workforce who adheres to Institute policies; equipped with state-of-the-art infrastructure; founded on relevant instruction, strong culture of Research & Extension and enhanced resource-generating programs producing highly competitive graduates imbued with professional values.

Mission

Transform the deserving sector of the society into highly competitive graduates who will respond to the needs of the Local and International Industries for ICT professionals. The College of Computer Studies is committed to:

  • Develop a highly motivated versatile ICT workforce
  • Offer highly specialized and ladderized programs
  • Keep abreast with state-of-the-art technology
  • Develop a strong culture of research, extension and production
Goals
  • Produce ICT graduates who are globally competitive, innovative, and ethically responsible
  • Strengthen research and extension programs that address local, national, and global ICT challenges
  • Establish and sustain partnerships with industry, government, and academic institutions
  • Continuously improve curricula to align with international standards, accreditation requirements, and emerging technologies
  • Foster a culture of academic excellence, professional integrity, and community engagement among faculty and students

Undergraduate Programs

Descriptions and objectives of CCS degree programs

BSIT
Bachelor of Science in Information Technology
Program Description

The BS Information Technology program prepares students to develop software applications across desktop, web, mobile, and various platforms, and to identify and implement practices for managing data infrastructure in IT. It also covers designing, implementing, and evaluating computer-based systems and processes to meet the information technology needs of industries. The program offers two specialization tracks: the Database Systems Track and the Network Systems Track.

Educational Philosophy

The BSIT program embraces outcomes-based education anchored in practical application and industry relevance. It cultivates a learning environment where students develop technical proficiency through hands-on projects, collaborative problem-solving, and real-world IT scenarios — preparing them to be adaptive, innovative, and responsible IT professionals ready to serve diverse industries.

Program Learning Objectives
  • Produce globally competent, innovative, and ethically responsible IT professionals capable of contributing to local, regional, and national development goals.
  • Develop software applications for desktop, web, mobile, and emerging platforms.
  • Design, implement, and evaluate computer-based systems and processes meeting industry IT needs.
  • Equip graduates with knowledge in data infrastructure management, database systems, and network systems.
  • Foster information literacy fundamentals and tools for a self-reliant, knowledge-based society.
BSIS
Bachelor of Science in Information Systems
Program Description

Information Systems is a computing discipline that complements both Computer Science and Information Technology. Of prime importance is its utility in the conceptualization, design, development, and evaluation of technical systems used in society. The BSIS program targets to empower students to associate information with business strategies and appropriate technologies for the benefit of particular organizations or business industries.

Educational Philosophy

The BSIS program is grounded in the integration of computing with business and organizational strategy. It fosters analytical thinking, systems design competence, and strategic IT decision-making through case-based learning, cross-disciplinary collaboration, and exposure to real organizational challenges — equipping graduates to bridge the gap between technology and business value.

Program Learning Objectives
  • Apply knowledge of business processes, computing, mathematics, and social sciences appropriate to the field of Information Systems.
  • Design, implement, and evaluate information systems that meet organizational needs and align IT strategy with business strategy.
  • Assist organizations in IT change management, technology adoption, and IS plan development.
  • Empower graduates to become IT strategists capable of contributing to the advancement of businesses and community development.
BSCS
Bachelor of Science in Computer Science
Program Description

The BS Computer Science program produces graduates with strong foundations in computing, mathematics, algorithms, and software engineering. Students gain the ability to analyze complex problems, design computer-based solutions, and apply mathematical and algorithmic principles in the modeling of systems. The program emphasizes both theoretical rigor and practical software design.

Educational Philosophy

The BSCS program is built on a philosophy of rigorous theoretical foundations complemented by practical software engineering skills. Through algorithmic thinking, mathematical reasoning, and iterative design, students are trained to approach complex computational problems with depth and precision — producing graduates who can advance the frontiers of computing while upholding ethical and professional standards.

Program Learning Objectives
  • Apply knowledge of computing, basic science, and mathematics appropriate to the discipline and the program educational objectives.
  • Analyze a problem, identify and define computing requirements appropriate to the problem's solution.
  • Apply mathematical foundations, algorithmic principles, and computer science theory in the modeling and design of computer-based systems.
  • Apply design principles in constructing software systems of varying complexity, in accordance with applicable standards.
  • Engage in productive practice as a computer science professional, adapt to technology advances, and make well-rounded decisions on social, ethical, legal, and environmental issues in computing.
BSCA
Bachelor of Science in Computer Applications
Program Description

The BS Computer Applications program leads in the field of emerging technologies, providing students with the knowledge and technical skills needed for Industry 4.0 implementation. With faculty specializing in computer applications and interfacing, electronic circuit analysis, computer networking, and hardware programming, the program offers two tracks: the Embedded Systems Track and the Internet of Things (IoT) Track.

Educational Philosophy

The BSCA program embraces a philosophy of innovation-driven learning at the intersection of hardware and software. Through experiential education in embedded systems, IoT, and hardware–software interfacing, students are empowered to design and prototype tangible technological solutions — fostering inventive thinking, rapid prototyping skills, and the ability to contribute patentable innovations to the ICT landscape.

Program Learning Objectives
  • Understand the design and development of hardware devices and software programs through specialized skills in Embedded Systems and Internet of Things (IoT).
  • Sharpen mathematical analysis and produce patentable innovations that cater to ICT demands in solving emerging and future global problems.
  • Apply skills in computer applications and hardware–software interfacing to develop research-driven solutions through rapid technological advancement.
  • Develop competencies in electronic circuit analysis, microcontroller-based hardware design, computer networking, and IoT application development.
